IoT Security in Medical Devices

Understanding IoT in Healthcare

IoT in the medical field refers to a network of connected devices that collect, transmit, and sometimes analyze patient data in real time.

Why IoT Medical Devices Are Vulnerable

These devices are low-powered, always connected, and not regularly updated, making them susceptible to attacks.

Real-World Cases of IoT Security Risks

  • FDA Warning (2019)
  • WannaCry Attack (2017)
  • Pacemaker Hacking (2017)

Key Security Challenges

Lack of standardization, device longevity, supply chain risks, and privacy concerns are key challenges.

Best Practices

Security-by-design, firmware updates, encryption, and training are essential to protect medical IoT devices.

The Road Ahead

With proper investment in IoT security, we can ensure that these life-saving technologies remain safe.
